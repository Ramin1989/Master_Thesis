## Project Title

**Conceptual Metaphors in Climate Change Communication:**\
**A Cross-Cultural Cognitive and Computational Study**

## Overview

This repository contains the code, data processing pipeline, and
analysis scripts used for my Master's thesis in **Cognitive Science**.
The thesis explores how **conceptual metaphors** shape public
understanding of climate change across different cultural contexts. It
combines insights from **linguistics**, **psychology**, and **data
science** to examine how figurative language influences perception and
persuasion in climate discourse.

The work reflects an interdisciplinary approach that leverages both
**qualitative theory** and **quantitative methods** to shed light on the
relationship between language and cognition in a pressing global issue.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Research Questions

- What conceptual metaphors dominate public conversations about climate
  change?
- How do these metaphors vary across cultures and languages?
- What cognitive effects do different metaphorical framings have on
  attitudes and decision making regarding climate policy?
- Can computational techniques help quantify metaphor usage and its
  impact?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Methodology

The thesis employs a mixed-methods approach:

- **Corpus analysis:** Collection and preprocessing of climate change
  texts from multiple languages to identify metaphorical patterns.
- **Annotation and coding:** Manual and semi-automated annotation of
  metaphor candidates, guided by conceptual metaphor theory.
- **Quantitative analysis:** Statistical comparison of metaphor
  frequencies and frames across cultural datasets.
- **Cognitive experiments:** Surveys or experiments assessing the
  persuasive impact of different metaphorical framings.
- **Visualization:** Visual summaries of metaphor distributions and
  cross-cultural differences.
